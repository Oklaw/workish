ABOUT

Why UpNext Works

We’re building a safe, local way for families to get help — and for teens to gain real-world experience — with clear guardrails and oversight baked in.

The need we’re filling

  • ✓ Families need trustworthy, short-duration help for yardwork, snow, pet care, cleaning, babysitting, and tutoring.
  • ✓ Teens (14–17) want flexible, nearby ways to earn money and practice responsibility without navigating risky gig platforms.
  • ✓ Existing marketplaces don’t center youth safety, parental oversight, or age-appropriate task controls. We do.

Benefits for customers & young adults

  • ✓ For customers: reliable, local help that’s verified and age-appropriate — with simple scheduling and feedback.
  • ✓ For teens: real experience, communication skills, and references for jobs/college — with short, safe tasks that fit school.
  • ✓ For parents/guardians: clear oversight and visibility into tasks, timing, and safety expectations.

Safety and parental controls — built in

  • ✓ Verified teen accounts with required parent/guardian involvement and contact preferences.
  • ✓ Age-appropriate task catalog with what’s allowed vs. not allowed clearly defined by age band.
  • ✓ Transparent job details, checklists, and the right to decline tasks that feel unsafe — no penalties.
  • ✓ Clear communication lanes and escalation guidance for both families and teens.

Designed for compliance with youth labor rules

  • ✓ Age-based restrictions surfaced at signup and enforced in task matching.
  • ✓ Guidance on Virginia youth-labor rules (e.g., work permits for 14–15, hour limits, equipment restrictions).
  • ✓ In-product nudges and disclosures to help ensure tasks and schedules align with applicable laws.
  • ✓ Ongoing updates as regulations evolve, with parents kept in the loop.

Note: UpNext Works is piloting with clearly defined task types and schedules, and will continue refining controls to support safe, lawful participation.

How it works

  • 1) Teens and a parent/guardian apply once — we verify details and set age-appropriate settings.
  • 2) Families request help; the system matches by proximity, availability, and allowed tasks.
  • 3) Task gets done with checklists and feedback — building trust and useful references over time.
